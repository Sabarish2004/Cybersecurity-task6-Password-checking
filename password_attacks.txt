1. Brute Force Attack:
   - The attacker tries every possible combination of characters to guess the password.
   - It is time-consuming but effective for weak or short passwords.

2. Dictionary Attack:
   - Uses a list of commonly used passwords or dictionary words.
   - Fast and effective if passwords are common or simple words.

3. Credential Stuffing:
   - Reuses stolen usernames and passwords from previous data breaches.
   - Effective because many people reuse passwords across different sites.

4. Rainbow Table Attack:
   - Uses precomputed hash values of passwords to crack them quickly.
   - Can be prevented using salted hashing techniques.

5. Phishing:
   - Tricks the user into revealing their password through fake websites or messages.
   - Often uses social engineering techniques.
