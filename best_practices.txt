1. Use a long password (at least 12 characters).
2. Include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
3. Avoid using personal information like your name or birthdate.
4. Do not reuse passwords across multiple accounts.
5. Use passphrases made up of random words (e.g., Red!Tiger_Cupboard2025).
6. Use a trusted password manager to store and generate passwords.
7. Regularly update your passwords, especially after a breach.
8. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible.
